错误处理与可观测性：信号架构

01 Core Insight — The Signal Architecture

Most teams treat error handling and observability as afterthoughts — bolted on after the "real" architecture is done. A try/catch here, a log.error() there, a dashboard assembled during the first postmortem. This is backwards. The way your system communicates failure is itself an architecture — one that requires the same deliberate design as your domain model or API contracts.

多数团队将错误处理和可观测性视为事后补丁——在"真正的"架构完成后才加上。try/catch这里一个，log.error()那里一个，仪表盘在第一次事后复盘时才拼凑。这是本末倒置的。系统传达失败的方式本身就是一种架构——需要与领域模型或API契约同等程度的刻意设计。

Michael Nygard's insight from Release It! is foundational: most production failures happen at integration points. Not in your business logic, not in your algorithms — at the boundaries where systems meet. In the AI era, this insight intensifies. AI services introduce integration points with entirely novel failure modes: hallucinations that return syntactically valid but semantically wrong data, latency distributions with fat tails, token limits that silently truncate context, and rate limits that materialize under load. Your signal architecture must detect, classify, and respond to failures that no one anticipated at design time.

Nygard的核心洞察：大多数生产故障发生在集成点。AI时代让这一洞察更加尖锐——AI服务引入了全新的故障模式：语法正确但语义错误的幻觉、肥尾延迟分布、静默截断上下文的token限制。你的信号架构必须检测、分类和响应设计时无人预见的故障。

The Reframing 核心重构

Errors are not exceptions — they are signals. A well-designed error is a structured message that tells three audiences what happened: the end user (what to do next), the operator (where to look), and the machine (how to auto-remediate). In the AI era, a fourth audience emerges: the AI system itself, which learns from error patterns to prevent future failures. Design your errors for all four consumers from day one.

错误不是异常——而是信号。设计良好的错误是结构化消息，同时告知三类受众发生了什么：终端用户（下一步做什么）、运维人员（去哪里排查）、机器（如何自动修复）。AI时代出现了第四类受众：AI系统本身，它从错误模式中学习以预防未来故障。

02 Classical Foundation — Source Books

Three books define the classical approach to error handling and production observability. The first teaches you to design for failure at integration points. The second transforms how you ask questions about your running systems. The third provides the operational framework — SLIs, SLOs, and error budgets — that turns reliability from a vague aspiration into a measurable engineering discipline.

三本书定义了错误处理和生产可观测性的经典方法。第一本教你在集成点设计容错。第二本变革你对运行系统提问的方式。第三本提供运维框架——SLI、SLO和错误预算——将可靠性从模糊愿望转变为可测量的工程纪律。

Release It! Design and Deploy Production-Ready Software, 2nd Ed
Michael Nygard · 2018
Stability patterns: Circuit Breaker, Bulkhead, Timeout, Retry. Anti-patterns: Cascading Failure, Integration Points, Chain Reactions. Key insight: most failures happen at integration points. In AI era: AI services are integration points with unique failure modes — hallucination, latency spikes, token limits.
FOUNDATION · STABILITY PATTERNS
Observability Engineering
Charity Majors, Liz Fong-Jones, George Miranda · 2022
The shift from monitoring (known-unknowns) to observability (unknown-unknowns). Three pillars: structured logs, distributed traces, metrics. High-cardinality, high-dimensionality data. Key insight: ask arbitrary questions of production without deploying new code.
FOUNDATION · OBSERVABILITY
Site Reliability Engineering
Betsy Beyer et al., Google · 2016
SRE practices: SLIs, SLOs, Error Budgets, Toil elimination. The error budget concept: spend your reliability budget wisely. In AI era: AI components consume error budget differently — a 1% hallucination rate vs a 1% downtime rate require fundamentally different treatment.
PRACTICE · SRE FRAMEWORK

What unites these three books is a shared conviction that production behavior is a first-class design concern, not an operational afterthought. Nygard designs the defenses (Circuit Breakers, Bulkheads), Majors provides the eyes (observability), and Beyer supplies the governance (error budgets). Together, they form the "signal architecture" — the subsystem that ensures your system can communicate its health, detect anomalies, and respond to failure before users notice. In the NRS context, this signal architecture must span multiple country tax authorities (Chile SII, Mexico SAT), internal microservices, and increasingly, AI-powered document processing pipelines.

三本书的共同信念：生产行为是第一类设计关注点，而非运维的事后考虑。Nygard设计防御（熔断器、舱壁），Majors提供视野（可观测性），Beyer提供治理（错误预算）。在NRS场景中，信号架构必须跨越多国税务机关、内部微服务以及AI文档处理管道。

03 AI Co-Evolution — Four Angles

The signal architecture extends naturally into the AI era. Each angle below follows the strict micro-format: one classical principle, one AI-era extension, one concrete example. The throughline: AI doesn't replace your observability stack — it becomes both a consumer and a subject of it.

信号架构自然延伸到AI时代。以下每个角度遵循严格的微格式：一个经典原则、一个AI时代延伸、一个具体示例。贯穿线索：AI不是替代你的可观测性栈——它同时成为其消费者和被观测对象。

Angle A · From Threshold Alerts to AI Anomaly Detection — 从阈值告警到AI异常检测

Classical principle: Set thresholds on metrics — CPU > 80%, error rate > 5%, latency p99 > 2s — and alert when exceeded. This is Nygard's operational awareness applied to metrics: you define the boundaries of normal, and the system screams when they are breached.

AI extension: Threshold alerts produce alert fatigue and miss slow-burn degradation. AI anomaly detection learns baseline patterns and flags deviations that humans would miss: "Error rate is 3% — below your 5% threshold — but it has been climbing 0.2%/hour for 6 hours, which is anomalous." More powerfully, AI correlates across signals that humans track in separate dashboards: a latency spike in Service A + error rate increase in Service B + a recent deploy in Service C suggests a root cause that no single threshold would catch.

Concrete example: Consider the NRS platform monitoring Chile SII integrations. A classical threshold alert fires only when the SII rejection rate exceeds 10%. An AI anomaly detector notices that rejections for a specific document type (Factura Exenta) climbed from 0.5% to 2.8% over four hours — well below threshold, but three standard deviations from its learned baseline. It correlates this with a SII API version change logged 6 hours prior, and surfaces the probable cause before any customer reports an issue.

经典原则：对指标设置阈值并告警。AI延伸：AI异常检测学习基线模式，发现人类会忽略的缓慢退化——"错误率3%低于5%阈值，但6小时内以0.2%/小时攀升，这是异常的。"AI还能跨信号关联：服务A延迟飙升+服务B错误率上升+服务C最近部署→AI推断根因。具体示例：NRS平台中AI检测到特定文档类型的SII拒绝率异常攀升，关联SII API版本变更，在用户投诉前定位问题。

Angle B · Structured Errors as Training Data — 结构化错误作为训练数据

Classical principle: Nygard's error handling patterns: categorize errors by type (transient vs permanent, client vs server), provide actionable error messages, and fail fast when recovery is impossible. An error response should tell the caller exactly what went wrong and what they can do about it.

AI extension: When errors are structured — JSON envelopes with error code, category, context fields, trace ID, and timestamp — they become a machine-readable knowledge base. Over time, AI learns correlations: "SII error code 99 with context CAF_SEQUENCE_EXHAUSTED and document type FACTURA has been resolved 47 out of 50 times by requesting a new CAF range from SII." The error catalog evolves from a human-readable reference into an AI training dataset. The critical design decision: structure your errors for machine consumption from day one, not just human reading.

经典原则：按类型分类错误，提供可操作的错误消息，无法恢复时快速失败。AI延伸：结构化错误（带错误码、类别、上下文、追踪ID的JSON信封）成为机器可读的知识库。AI学习关联："SII错误码99+CAF序列耗尽，94%的情况下通过请求新CAF范围解决。"关键设计决策：从第一天起为机器消费设计错误结构。

Angle C · Auto-Remediation Pipelines — 自动修复管道

Classical principle: Runbooks — documented step-by-step procedures that human operators follow to resolve known incident types. Google's SRE book formalizes this: every on-call rotation should have runbooks for the top 10 known failure modes, and toil (repetitive manual work) should be systematically eliminated.

AI extension: AI reads runbooks and executes auto-remediation for known error patterns, progressively reducing human involvement. Circuit Breaker trips? AI checks downstream service health, verifies the root cause is transient, and resets the breaker after confirmation. SII returns a rate-limit error? AI implements exponential backoff, redistributes the queue across time windows, and alerts the team only if the backlog grows beyond SLO. Novel, never-seen-before errors? AI escalates to a human with a context summary, similar past incidents, and three suggested actions ranked by historical success rate.

Level 0 — No Runbooks
Senior engineer's tribal knowledge. They wake up at 3am, SSH into production, and figure it out.
Level 1 — Documented Runbooks
Step-by-step procedures in a wiki. Any on-call engineer can follow them. Still manual execution.
Level 2 — Scripted Remediation
Runbooks translated to scripts. Human triggers execution. ./fix-caf-exhaustion.sh --country CL
Level 3 — Alert-Triggered Automation
Alert fires → script runs automatically for known patterns. Human reviews outcome. PagerDuty + Rundeck.
Level 4 — AI-Assisted Remediation (2025→)
AI reads error context + runbook + past incidents. Selects and executes remediation. Escalates unknowns with suggested actions and confidence scores.
Level 5 — AI-Autonomous Operations (2026→)
Full loop: AI detects anomaly → diagnoses root cause → executes remediation → verifies fix → writes postmortem → updates runbook. Human oversight shifts from approval to audit.

经典原则：Runbook——运维人员解决已知故障的文档化步骤。AI延伸：AI读取Runbook并对已知错误模式执行自动修复。熔断器触发→AI检查下游健康→确认是瞬态故障→自动重置。未知错误→AI携带上下文摘要、相似历史事件和三个建议操作升级给人类。进化路径从"3AM的SSH"到"AI自主运维"。

Angle D · Observability for AI Components — AI组件的可观测性

Classical principle: The three pillars of observability — structured logs, distributed traces, and metrics — give you the ability to ask arbitrary questions about your production system's behavior. Majors' key insight: observability is not about dashboards you built in advance; it's about being able to explore dimensions you didn't anticipate.

AI extension: AI components need additional observability dimensions that traditional services never required. A database query either succeeds or fails; an AI inference can succeed syntactically but fail semantically (hallucination). Traditional latency is network + compute; AI latency includes prompt tokenization, model inference, and output decoding — with distributions that look nothing like your typical p50/p99 curves. Your observability stack must treat AI components as first-class citizens with their own SLIs and SLOs.

Dimension CLASSIC Traditional SLI AI+ AI-Specific SLI
Correctness
正确性		 HTTP 2xx rate, data integrity checks Hallucination rate, semantic correctness score, factual grounding rate
Latency
延迟		 Request p50/p95/p99 in milliseconds Time-to-first-token, total inference time, prompt/completion token ratio
Cost
成本		 CPU/memory utilization per request Token usage per request, cost per inference, cache hit rate for RAG
Availability
可用性		 Uptime percentage, successful request rate Model endpoint availability + rate limit headroom + fallback model health

经典原则：可观测性三大支柱让你能对生产系统提出任意问题。AI延伸：AI组件需要传统服务从未需要的额外可观测维度——幻觉率（语义正确性）、token用量（成本追踪）、置信度分布、RAG缓存命中率。将AI组件视为拥有自身SLI/SLO的一等公民。

06 The Architect's Takeaway

Errors and observability are not afterthoughts — they are architecture. The "signal architecture" — the subsystem that detects, classifies, communicates, and responds to failure — deserves the same deliberate design attention as your domain model, your API contracts, and your data schema. Nygard's stability patterns, Majors' observability framework, and Google's SRE practices form the classical foundation. The AI era extends this foundation with anomaly detection that sees what humans miss, structured errors that train future remediation, and auto-remediation pipelines that close the loop from detection to resolution without human intervention.

错误和可观测性不是事后补丁——它们是架构。信号架构——检测、分类、传达和响应故障的子系统——值得与领域模型、API契约和数据模式同等的设计关注。Nygard的稳定性模式、Majors的可观测性框架、Google的SRE实践构成经典基础。AI时代以异常检测、结构化错误训练和自动修复管道扩展了这一基础。
