Architecture Styles Landscape

架构风格全景：从单体到AI原生
Article 2.1 of 20 ~20 min read 4 Source Books × 4 AI Angles

01 Core Insight — The Thesis

Architecture style is the highest-leverage decision a software team makes. Choose monolith when you need microservices, and you'll drown in a distributed ball of mud. Choose microservices when a modular monolith would suffice, and you'll spend years paying a "distribution tax" — network serialization, eventual consistency, deployment orchestration — that buys you nothing. The books agree: there are no best architectures, only trade-offs made explicit.

架构风格是软件团队做出的最高杠杆决策。在需要微服务时选择单体，你将淹没在分布式泥球中。在模块化单体足够时选择微服务，你将花数年支付毫无回报的"分布税"。这些书的共识：没有最佳架构，只有被显式化的权衡。

Here is the AI-era twist: AI doesn't change which architecture style is right for your context — it changes how safely and quickly you can evolve between styles. A modular monolith with AI-enforced boundaries can remain a monolith longer without accumulating the coupling that historically forced premature decomposition. When decomposition finally becomes necessary, AI-assisted analysis of module boundaries, data affinity, and communication patterns makes the extraction dramatically less risky. The spectrum of styles hasn't changed; the cost of moving along it has dropped by an order of magnitude.

AI时代的转折：AI不会改变哪种架构风格适合你的场景——它改变的是你在风格之间演进的安全性和速度。AI执行的模块边界让单体可以更久地保持单体形态，而当分解真正必要时，AI辅助分析使提取风险大幅降低。风格光谱没变，沿光谱移动的成本降低了一个数量级。

The Reframing 核心重构

Architecture style is a spectrum, not a binary choice. Classical books gave us the taxonomy — monolith, SOA, microservices, event-driven, serverless. AI adds a new position: "AI-native architecture" where intelligent agents are first-class architectural components, not bolted-on features. But more importantly, AI transforms the transitions between styles. The question shifts from "which style should we pick forever?" to "where are we on the spectrum today, and what evidence would trigger movement?"

架构风格是一个光谱，不是二元选择。经典书籍给出了分类法，AI添加了新位置："AI原生架构"。但更重要的是，AI改变了风格之间的转换。问题从"我们该永远选哪种风格"变为"我们今天在光谱上的哪个位置，什么证据会触发移动？"

02 Classical Foundation — Source Books

Four books form the intellectual bedrock of architecture style decisions. Two address the taxonomy and trade-off analysis directly; two provide the structural principles and evolutionary mechanisms that make style transitions possible. Together they cover the full decision chain: which style to choose, how to structure it internally, and how to evolve it over time.

四本书构成架构风格决策的知识基石。两本直接处理分类法和权衡分析，两本提供使风格转换成为可能的结构原则和演进机制。它们共同覆盖完整的决策链：选择哪种风格、如何在内部组织、以及如何随时间演进。

Software Architecture: The Hard Parts
Ford, Richards, Sadalage, Dehghani · 2021
Trade-off analysis for architecture decisions. Modularity drivers, data decomposition, distributed workflow patterns. Key insight: there are no right answers in architecture, only trade-offs. The book provides analytical frameworks for making those trade-offs explicit and defensible.
FOUNDATION · TRADE-OFF ANALYSIS
Fundamentals of Software Architecture
Richards & Ford · 2020
Comprehensive taxonomy of architecture styles: layered, microkernel, event-driven, microservices, space-based, service-based. Defines architecture characteristics (the "-ilities") and introduces fitness functions for evolutionary architecture. The ADR pattern for documenting decisions.
FOUNDATION · TAXONOMY
Clean Architecture
Robert C. Martin · 2017
The Dependency Rule: dependencies point inward. Entities, Use Cases, Interface Adapters, Frameworks — concentric rings where policy is protected from detail. In the AI era, AI components are "details" (outer ring); domain logic remains the inviolable core.
PRACTICE · DEPENDENCY RULE
Building Evolutionary Architectures
Ford, Parsons, Kua · 2017
Fitness functions: automated checks that architecture characteristics are maintained over time. Guided, incremental change. The architecture quantum — the smallest independently deployable unit with high functional cohesion. AI can now evaluate fitness functions continuously.
PRACTICE · EVOLUTIONARY ARCH

What connects these four books is a shared conviction that architecture is not a one-time decision but a continuous evolution. Ford and Richards give you the language for discussing trade-offs. Martin gives you the structural invariant (the Dependency Rule) that holds regardless of which style you choose. Ford, Parsons, and Kua give you the mechanism for ensuring your architecture stays healthy as it evolves. The AI era doesn't invalidate any of this — it accelerates the feedback loops, lowers the cost of conformance checking, and makes the "evolutionary" part of evolutionary architecture actually practical for teams without dedicated architecture review boards.

这四本书的共同信念：架构不是一次性决策，而是持续演进。Ford和Richards提供权衡讨论的语言，Martin提供无论选择哪种风格都成立的结构不变量（依赖规则），Ford、Parsons和Kua提供确保架构健康演进的机制。AI时代没有使这些失效——它加速了反馈循环，降低了合规检查成本，让演进式架构对没有专门架构审查委员会的团队变得切实可行。

03 AI Co-Evolution — Four Angles

Each angle below takes one classical architecture principle and extends it with an AI capability that shifts the economics of architecture decisions. The pattern: classical principle provides the what and why; AI provides the how continuously and how safely.

以下每个角度取一个经典架构原则，用AI能力扩展它，改变架构决策的经济学。模式：经典原则提供"做什么"和"为什么"；AI提供"如何持续"和"如何安全"。

Angle A · The Architecture Style Spectrum in 2025 — 2025年架构风格光谱

Classical principle: Richards and Ford (Fundamentals of Software Architecture) provide a comprehensive taxonomy: layered, microkernel, pipeline, event-driven, space-based, service-based, microservices. Each style has an architecture characteristics star chart — how well it supports elasticity, fault tolerance, scalability, simplicity, etc. The choice depends on which "-ilities" matter most for your domain.

AI extension: The 2025 spectrum adds a new position: "AI-native architecture" where AI agents are first-class architectural components, not bolted-on features. Characteristics of AI-native: stateless compute with stateful context (vector stores, conversation memory), event-driven by default (agents react to events, not just HTTP requests), contract-first APIs (agents need explicit schemas to function), and observability as architecture (every agent call is traced, every decision is logged). This isn't a separate style — it's an evolution of event-driven + microservices that treats intelligence as a distributed capability.

Monolith — Single Process, Single Database
Everything in one deployable unit. Simple to develop and test initially. Coupling accumulates silently. Works for small teams and early-stage products.
Modular Monolith — Boundaries Without Distribution
Single deploy unit with enforced module boundaries. Internal APIs between modules. The "sweet spot" for most teams — monolith simplicity with service-level modularity.
Service-Based — Coarse-Grained Services
4-12 domain-aligned services. Shared database still possible. Independent deployability without full microservice operational overhead.
Microservices — Full Distribution
Database per service. Independent deployment, scaling, and technology choice. High operational cost. Only justified when independent deployability is a hard requirement.
Serverless / Event-Driven — Function-Level Granularity
Functions triggered by events. Maximum elasticity, pay-per-use. Complex orchestration, cold starts, vendor lock-in. Natural fit for event-heavy AI workloads.
AI-Native — Agents as First-Class Components (2025→)
AI agents integrated into the architecture fabric. Contract-first APIs, event-driven backbone, observability-native, stateless compute with stateful context stores. Intelligence as a distributed architectural capability.

经典原则：Richards和Ford提供了完整的架构风格分类法，每种风格有特征星图。AI延伸：2025年光谱增加了"AI原生架构"——AI代理作为一等架构组件。特征包括：无状态计算+有状态上下文、默认事件驱动、契约优先API、可观测性即架构。

Angle B · AI as Architecture Fitness Function Evaluator — AI作为架构适应度函数评估器

Classical principle: Ford, Parsons, and Kua introduced fitness functions in Building Evolutionary Architectures — automated checks that verify architecture characteristics are maintained over time. Examples: "no cyclic dependencies between modules," "p99 latency under 200ms," "no direct database access from the presentation layer." These are the immune system of your architecture.

AI extension: Classical fitness functions are rule-based — you can check cyclomatic complexity or dependency direction with static analysis. But many architecture characteristics are "soft" — coherence of module responsibilities, adherence to stated principles in ADRs, semantic coupling between bounded contexts. AI can evaluate these soft fitness functions that were previously manual. An AI agent reads your ADR history, your module structure, and a pull request diff, then answers: "Does this PR increase coupling between the Order and Inventory bounded contexts?" or "Does this change violate ADR-012 which states that all inter-service communication must be asynchronous?" This transforms architecture governance from periodic manual review to continuous automated evaluation.

Concrete example: A CI pipeline that runs AI fitness function evaluation on every pull request. Step 1: traditional static analysis (ArchUnit, dependency-cruiser). Step 2: AI reads the PR diff against the .ai/architecture.md and the ADR directory, evaluating semantic conformance. Step 3: AI produces a structured verdict — PASS, WARN, or BLOCK — with citations to specific ADRs or architecture principles that the change supports or violates.

经典原则：适应度函数——验证架构特征随时间维护的自动化检查。AI延伸：AI可以评估"软"适应度函数——模块职责一致性、ADR原则遵循、语义耦合——这些以前只能人工评审。AI读取ADR历史和PR差异，持续判断变更是否违反架构原则。具体示例：CI管道在每个PR上运行AI适应度函数评估，产出PASS/WARN/BLOCK裁定。

Angle C · The Modular Monolith Renaissance + AI — 模块化单体的文艺复兴

Classical principle: Ford, Richards, Sadalage, and Dehghani (The Hard Parts, Ch. 2-3) make a compelling case: the modular monolith gives you the best of both worlds for many teams. Deploy as one unit but maintain strict module boundaries. You get monolith simplicity (one database, one deployment pipeline, no distributed transactions) with service-level modularity (clear APIs between modules, independent team ownership, future extractability). The key requirement is discipline — without enforcement, module boundaries erode under deadline pressure.

AI extension: AI makes the modular monolith MORE viable by solving the discipline problem. ArchUnit and similar tools enforce structural rules (no package X importing from package Y). AI adds semantic enforcement: it understands why a boundary exists, not just where it is. AI detects coupling drift before it becomes architectural erosion — "Module A is starting to reach into Module B's internal data model; this wasn't the case 30 days ago." When decomposition finally becomes necessary, AI assists by analyzing module boundaries, data affinity, and communication patterns to recommend the optimal extraction sequence. The mantra: "Start monolith, extract when data shows you must" becomes dramatically safer with AI guardrails.

◆ Modular Monolith with AI Boundary Enforcement

经典原则：模块化单体——单一部署单元但维持严格模块边界，兼得单体简单性和服务级模块化。AI延伸：AI通过语义执行解决纪律问题——不仅知道边界在哪里，还理解边界为什么存在。AI检测耦合漂移，在分解真正必要时辅助分析最优提取序列。口诀："以单体开始，当数据表明必须时再提取"——有了AI护栏这变得更安全。

Angle D · Architecture Decision Records Meet AI — 架构决策记录遇见AI

Classical principle: Michael Nygard introduced Architecture Decision Records (ADRs) — lightweight documents that capture the why behind architecture choices. Each ADR records: Status, Context (the forces at play), Decision (the choice made), and Consequences (the trade-offs accepted). Richards and Ford (Fundamentals) elevated ADRs to a core architecture practice. The power of ADRs is institutional memory — when a new team member asks "why did we choose event sourcing for order processing?", the answer is in ADR-007, not in a departed architect's head.

AI extension: AI transforms ADRs from passive documentation into active architectural guardrails. First, AI consumes ADRs as context for code review and generation. When a developer opens a PR that introduces synchronous REST calls in the order processing pipeline, AI flags: "ADR-007 states that all order processing communication must be asynchronous via the event bus. This PR introduces a synchronous POST to /inventory/reserve. Please reconcile or propose an ADR amendment." Second, AI writes ADRs by analyzing PR discussions, Slack threads, and meeting notes — drafting the ADR for human review. Third, AI validates ADR consistency across the corpus — detecting conflicts between decisions and flagging when new ADRs implicitly supersede old ones without explicit deprecation. 

# ADR-007: Asynchronous Communication for Order Processing

## Status
Accepted — 2025-01-15
Supersedes: ADR-003 (synchronous order pipeline)

## Context
Order processing involves 4 bounded contexts: Order, Inventory,
Payment, Notification. Synchronous chains caused cascading failures
during Black Friday 2024 (incident INC-2847). P99 latency exceeded
SLA by 340%.

## Decision
All inter-module communication in the order processing flow will use
asynchronous events via Kafka. Each module publishes domain events;
consumers are idempotent. Saga pattern for distributed transactions.

## Consequences
+ Resilient to individual module failures
+ Independent scaling per module
- Eventual consistency (UI must handle pending states)
- Operational complexity of Kafka cluster
- Debugging distributed flows requires correlation IDs

## AI Integration
- CI fitness function: flag any synchronous HTTP call in
  packages matching com.example.order.*
- AI reviewer context: include this ADR when reviewing PRs
  touching order, inventory, payment, or notification modules
- Auto-link: any PR referencing INC-2847 should cite this ADR

经典原则：ADR记录架构决策的"为什么"——状态、上下文、决策、后果。AI延伸：AI将ADR从被动文档转化为主动架构护栏。AI消费ADR作为代码审查上下文，标记违反声明原则的PR。AI通过分析PR讨论和Slack线程起草ADR供人类审查。AI验证ADR语料库的一致性，检测决策之间的冲突。

Architecture style decisions are among the hardest to reverse. This table maps eight common scenarios to both classical guidance and AI-augmented approaches, showing how AI shifts the decision economics rather than the decision itself.

架构风格决策是最难逆转的决策之一。此表将八个常见场景映射到经典指导和AI增强方法，展示AI如何改变决策经济学而非决策本身。

Scenario 场景 CLASSIC Classical Approach AI+ AI-Augmented Approach
Monolith vs Microservices
单体 vs 微服务		 Evaluate team size, deployment cadence, scaling needs. Use architecture characteristics star chart. Start monolith unless strong distribution requirement exists. Same evaluation + AI continuously monitors coupling metrics. AI flags when modules exceed coupling threshold, recommending extraction only when data supports it. Delays premature decomposition.
Sync vs Async Communication
同步 vs 异步		 Synchronous for request-response queries. Asynchronous for commands with eventual consistency tolerance. Document in ADR. AI analyzes call chains and latency data to identify synchronous bottlenecks. AI fitness function blocks PRs introducing sync calls where ADR mandates async. AI suggests saga patterns for complex flows.
DB per Service vs Shared DB
服务独立库 vs 共享库		 Shared DB for modular monolith. Separate schemas per module as first step. DB per service only with microservices. Accept eventual consistency trade-off. AI analyzes query patterns across module boundaries to quantify data coupling. AI recommends schema separation sequence based on actual data affinity, not assumptions.
Event Sourcing vs CRUD
事件溯源 vs CRUD		 CRUD by default. Event sourcing only for audit-heavy domains (finance, healthcare) or when full history replay is a requirement. High implementation complexity. Same decision criteria + AI assists with event store implementation patterns. AI generates projection builders and replay handlers. AI validates event schema evolution for backward compatibility.
Serverless vs Containers
无服务器 vs 容器		 Serverless for event-driven, bursty workloads. Containers for steady-state, long-running processes. Consider cold start latency, vendor lock-in, and cost model. GPU resource management as first-class concern.

06 The Architect's Takeaway

The architecture style choice is the highest-leverage decision you will make on any system. Get it wrong and everything downstream — team structure, data model, deployment pipeline, operational burden — suffers compounding costs. The classical books are not optional reading; they are the vocabulary you need to think clearly about trade-offs. Ford and Richards' taxonomy, Martin's Dependency Rule, and the fitness function concept from evolutionary architecture are not academic exercises — they are daily decision tools.

架构风格选择是你对任何系统做出的最高杠杆决策。选错了，下游一切——团队结构、数据模型、部署管道、运维负担——都会承受复利成本。经典书籍不是选修，它们是你清晰思考权衡所需的词汇表。

AI doesn't change which style is right for your context — it changes how safely you can evolve between styles. A modular monolith with AI-enforced boundaries is a fundamentally different proposition than a modular monolith held together by code review discipline alone. AI fitness functions transform architecture governance from periodic committee reviews to continuous automated evaluation. AI-consumed ADRs transform institutional memory from shelfware into active guardrails. The architecture is still yours to design; AI is the tooling that makes evolutionary architecture practically achievable.

AI不改变哪种风格适合你的场景——它改变你在风格之间演进的安全程度。有AI执行边界的模块化单体与仅靠代码审查纪律维持的模块化单体是根本不同的命题。AI适应度函数将架构治理从定期委员会审查转变为持续自动化评估。架构仍由你设计；AI是使演进式架构切实可行的工具。
